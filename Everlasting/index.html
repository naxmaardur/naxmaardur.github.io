Everlasting

In this project I was responsible for the Atmospheric Design, throughout the development of Everlasting I changed and tweaked many different things to make the experience give the feeling that we intended. The main things changed are sound and small visuals like the look of the eyes and adding rain. Adding the rain was a suggestion from another team member which I took and adapted. The rain in Everlasting starts out very intense and becomes weaker until it's fully gone at the climax if the story.

Story and mechanics

In Everlasting, you play as a young girl who lost her parents in a recent landslide, she is trying to record everything that is important to her while climbing her way up to the lighthouse of this small village. On her way there she encounters different supernatural creatures and a friendly cat that guids her to the lighthouse.
The game features two main mechanics
Taking pictures using the camera to record the happenings of the game
And a "sanity" system which makes your character panic more and more the longer you are in the darkness leading. This is represented by slightly warping the screen, making eyes look at you from the darkness and fully changing the audio that you hear.
